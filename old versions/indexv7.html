Why this exists

Evidence + compounding

Nobody can beat the market consistently. Still, there is excellent long-term data (over a century) and a small set of ideas that are surprisingly stable in the literature.

  • The core knowledge is public and documented in scientific work (e.g., Fama, Shiller) and rigorous books (e.g., Bernstein).
  • It’s not complicated. In one focused session, you can learn what is known, what is uncertain, and how to build a plan you can keep for decades.
  • This is for long-term investing (10+ years). Compounding is slow at first—and then becomes powerful.
  • Hiring a bank or robo-advisor is often hiring convenience, not “secret market knowledge”. Their recurring asset-based fees compound against you over time.
  • Tipsters and frequent trading typically underperform after costs; evidence is strong across multiple markets and time periods.

Learning macroeconomics is part of the point

Beyond portfolio mechanics, it’s genuinely fascinating to understand how macroeconomics shapes the world: inflation, interest rates, bonds vs stocks, why countries grow, demographics, productivity, and how companies are valued. This service is intended for people who want to learn deeply and handle their wealth with confidence— a particularly good fit for scientists and quantitative thinkers (ETH Zürich is full of them).

References (selected)

Public evidence (not promises)
  1. Fama, E. F. (1970). Efficient Capital Markets: A Review of Theory and Empirical Work. Journal of Finance.
  2. Fama, E. F., & French, K. R. (1992). The Cross-Section of Expected Stock Returns. Journal of Finance.
  3. Shiller, R. J. (1981). Do Stock Prices Move Too Much to Be Justified by Subsequent Changes in Dividends? American Economic Review.
  4. Bernstein, W. J. (2002). The Four Pillars of Investing. McGraw-Hill.
  5. Barber, B. M., & Odean, T. (2000). Trading Is Hazardous to Your Wealth: The Common Stock Investment Performance of Individual Investors. Journal of Finance.
The point: long-run evidence is strong, but outcomes are uncertain. This service is about understanding what is known, building a plan you can follow, and avoiding unnecessary compounding fees.
