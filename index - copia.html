Learn how to manage your wealth

Set a course you can follow for decades: understand markets, build a plan, and avoid compounding percentage fees.

Why this exists

Markets are not magic — and you don’t need an expensive intermediary to participate.

Nobody can reliably beat the market. But we do have more than a century of data and a small set of robust ideas documented in public research (Fama, Shiller, Bernstein, and others). This service is for people who want to learn the evidence and handle their wealth with clarity.

The core knowledge is public. You can learn it in a focused session and apply it for decades.
Long-term investing (10+ years) is where compounding becomes meaningful.
Bank and robo-advisor fees compound against you over time. You are often paying for convenience, not superior knowledge.
Trading and tipsters typically underperform after costs; the evidence is strong across markets and time periods.
Macroeconomics is worth learning: inflation, rates, bonds vs stocks, growth, demographics, productivity, valuation.
Simple beats complex. Thanks to academic finance and quantitative thinking, the essentials are surprisingly simple. Whoever makes it complicated often does so because complexity justifies ongoing fees.

Who is this for

For people who want to learn — and take control.

This is an educational service designed for long-term, evidence-based investing. It’s built for people who want clarity, not stories — and who want to handle their investments themselves.

Good fit

You want to learn how markets work and how to build a plan you can follow.
You have a quantitative/scientific interest and prefer evidence over opinions.
You want long-term, secure results (10+ years) rather than short-term excitement.
You want autonomy: you’ll own the process and keep fees low.

Not a fit

You like betting, speculation, “tips”, or irrational investing.
You don’t want to learn or you want to delegate everything permanently.
You want zero work. This is not a lot of work — but it’s not zero.
You expect guaranteed outperformance. That’s not how markets work.
Expectations (honest version): you won’t “get rich” quickly. But over the long term, a disciplined plan can realistically help you double your money — often roughly half from your savings and half from compounding. More than that tends to require more risk, more luck, or marketing promises.

What to expect

Small frictions compound into large outcomes.

The market is likely more expensive than it used to be, which means future returns may be lower than in some past decades. Still, a conservative assumption of ~5% annual real return (after inflation) is a reasonable baseline for long-term planning. It’s not “exciting” — but it’s among the best expected outcomes available without taking concentrated risks. The key is that it compounds.
